Building a Healthy Diet

  • Healthy Diet Portions
  1. 1 cup of fresh vegetables or fruit
  2. 1/2 cup of starchy vegetables or dried beans
  3. 1 slice of bread
  4. 1 cup of dry cereal or ½ cup cooked cereal
  5. 1/3 cup of rice or pasta
  6. 1 cup of low-fat milk
  7. 3 ounces of lean meat, chicken, or fish

Foods to eat

  1. Whole grains: brown rice, oats,etc.
  2. Fruits: berries, peaches, apples, pears, melons, bananas, grapes, etc.
  3. Nuts, nut butters, and seeds:sunflower seeds, pine nuts, and natural nut butters
  4. Eggs: organic, whole eggs are the healthiest and most nutrient dense
  5. Healthy fats: avocados, coconut oil, avocado oil, olive oil, etc.
  6. Calorie-free beverages: black coffee, tea, sparkling water, etc.

Foods to avoid

  1. Added sugars: agave, baked goods, ice cream, candy, etc. — limit added sugars to less than 5–10% of your total calories
  2. Fast food: French fries, hot dogs, pizza, chicken nuggets, etc.
  3. Processed and refined carbs: bagels, white bread, crackers, cookies, chips, sugary cereals, boxed pasta, etc.
  4. Fried foods: French fries, fried chicken, doughnuts, potato chips, fish and chips, etc.
  5. Sodas and sugar-sweetened beverages: sports drinks, sugary juices, sodas, fruit punch, sweetened tea and coffee drinks, etc.
  6. Diet and low-fat foods: diet ice cream, diet boxed snacks, diet packaged and frozen meals, and artificial sweeteners, such as Sweet n’ Low, etc.
